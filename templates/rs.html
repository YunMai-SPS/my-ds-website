Introduction

Tired of endless scrolling and review fatigue? Imagine a restaurant recommendation app that understands your preferences and serves up the perfect spot in seconds. No more decision paralysis, just delicious discoveries.

This project tackles that challenge head-on, leverages the power of a comprehensive user-review dataset and cutting-edge algorithms, honed in the data-driven insights of the course "Current Topics of Data Science: Recommendation Systems," to craft a personalized dining guide. Say goodbye to guesswork and hello to culinary bliss!

Aims:

  • Leveraging collaborative filtering: We'll utilize advanced algorithms to analyze user-restaurant interactions and predict preferences, building a robust foundation for personalized recommendations.
  • Enriching beyond ratings: Recognizing the limitations of solely relying on star ratings, we'll incorporate user feedback on humor, usefulness, and coolness. This multi-faceted approach aims to diversify and personalize recommendations, leading to serendipitous discoveries and potentially exceeding expectations.

Data:

The analysis is fueled by Yelp's Dataset Challenge round 9, a massive collection of user interactions and business attributes. With 4.1 million reviews and 947,000 tips contributed by 1 million users, the dataset paints a comprehensive picture of 144,000 businesses across various locations. Beyond reviews, the dataset dives deeper with 1.1 million business attributes like hours, parking, and ambiance, as well as aggregated check-ins for 125,000 businesses, providing valuable insights into user behavior and preferences.

Creating a Recommender

Building a user-based model involves constructing a "neighborhood" of similar users for each individual. By analyzing these neighborhoods, the model will learn how users with similar preferences tend to rate restaurants. This knowledge empowers it to predict how the target user would rate restaurants not yet experienced, ultimately recommending those most likely to resonate with their unique palate.

Building a User-based Collaborative Filtering Model: Single_Criterion v.s. Multi-Criteria Recommender System.

Conclusion and Discussion:

1. This project explored two approaches to restaurant recommendation systems: user-based collaborative filtering and multi-criteria ratings. Using the Yelp Dataset Challenge round 9 data, we successfully built a user-based model with an RMSE of 1.47. While this demonstrates the model's ability to learn user preferences, incorporating additional factors like location (e.g., distance or geospatial data) could further refine recommendations and improve personalization.

2. Intriguingly, the multi-criteria rating system yielded a completely different set of recommendations for users, with a 100% serendipity score. This suggests the system effectively diversifies recommendations beyond user-based similarities. Furthermore, the accuracy also improved, as evidenced by a significantly lower RMSE of 0.5. This suggests that utilizing multiple criteria, such as "funny," "useful," and "cool" in addition to ratings, can lead to more accurate and personalized recommendations.
