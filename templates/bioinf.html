CASE-1: COVID-19 immune profile and its role in COVID-19 pathology

Innate immune responses, including the production of type I and III interferon (IFN), impaired NK cytotoxicity, cytokine production, and lymphopenia, alongside adaptive immune responses like elevated neutrophils, increased inflammatory monocytes, and higher levels of pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-6 and GM-CSF), as well as excessive T-cell activation (HLA-DR, CD38, CD69, CD25, CD44, Ki-67, OX40, and CD137 by CD4+ and CD8+ T cells), have been reported to associated with disease severity in COVID-19. Analyzing high-dimensional cytometry data to identify known or novel cell populations and discriminate the differential expression of biomarkers will contribute to understanding the COVID-19 immune profile and its role in COVID-19 pathology.

Differential analysis of marker expression stratified by cell population

Differential analysis of marker expression stratified by cell population Results of the differential states test reveal the different expression of signaling markers within various cell populations in healthy controls and COVID‐19 patients in the ICU (Figure A). In Figure B, the normalized expression of signaling markers is averaged and compared between healthy controls and COVID‐19 patients in the ICU. Additionally, Figure C displays the comparison of signaling marker expression in COVID‐19 patients in the ICU and patients transitioning from the ICU to the ward.

Certain markers exhibit increased levels in COVID‐19 patients in the ICU: CCR7, CTLA-4, IL-4, IL-10, and CD25 show elevated expression in CD4+CD8- T cells, while CCR7 and IL-10 are elevated in CD8+CD4- T cells. PD-1 is upregulated in CD4+CD8+ T cells, and IL-10, CD25, IL-4, and CTLA-4 show elevated levels in CD14+ monocytes. Additionally, IL-17a and CD25 are increased in CD19+ B cells. Conversely, certain markers decrease in COVID‐19 patients in the ICU, with 4-1BB, IFNg, IL-6, and HLA-DR showing decreased expression in FOXP3 regulatory T cells. IFNg decreases in CD4+CD8- and CD8+CD4- T cells, 4-1BB decreases in CD19+ B cells, and IFNg decreases in CD14+ monocytes.
